Sierra Wireless Innovation Summit is above all a platform for exchange between the technical developer community and the IoT business and marketing community. This year, open hardware themes will take up a substantial part of the agenda. Be prepared for big announcements by Sierra Wireless and partners!

Throughout the day, partners and customers of Sierra Wireless will give live demos, present real use cases, and make announcements.

In the afternoon, the technical track offers hands-on keyboard sessions which allow developers to build a simple IoT end-to-end application using open hardware, open source software and an M2M cloud service. The business track will feature real commercial use cases and passionate discussions on future IoT technologies.

The agenda also makes ample room for networking and demos, allowing business and technical attendees to exchange, learn, and build the future of IoT.

   Smart Lighting and Clermont-Ferrand: connecting the city!
BUSINESS TRACK - 14h30/14h45
Clermont-Ferrand is the first European city with a fully connected Street Lighting network. With over 200 electrical cabinets and 16 000 light points connected, Clermont-Ferrand authorities succeeded in less than 18 months to over-secure the people, to strongly reduce energy and maintenance costs, and to drive the city on a long-term process of sustainable development. The extreme reliability, flexibility and simplicity of the Tegis solution optimizes street lighting management in accordance with the guidelines of the European standard EN13201: lighting control, monitoring and configuration, network monitoring, analysis and monitoring in real time.

Svetlana Grant

Project Director, Future IoT Networks, Connected Living Program

GSMA | gsma.com
Svetlana has over 15 years of experience in mobile industry. She has been a part of the GSMA’s Connected Living Program since 2009, working on the development of the Embedded Mobile Guidelines.    GSMA Initiatives for Mobilising the IoT
BUSINESS TRACK - 15h00/15h15(
The IoT market is evolving, and this means important changes in technology and services requirements: new frameworks for IoT begin to emerge, the networks begin to evolve in order to support low power low cost low data services, and the industry is working to manage and secure millions of new low cost devices. This presentation will discuss a host of new roles that will be played by mobile and introduce the GSMA industry initiatives that aim to support the growth of the Internet of Things.

   Wireless Sensor Networks for the Industrial Internet of Things
BUSINESS TRACK - 17h00/17h15
The Internet of Things (IoT) encompasses a very broad variety of applications, from consumer apps to smart cities to industrial efficiency. The wireless sensor network (WSN) requirements for connectivity in each of these applications are different. The specific WSN requirements and for industrial IoT applications are explored in this presentation, and examples of industrial IOT applications utilizing Dust Networks SmartMesh WSN products are provided.

Yves Portalier

GM & VP Telecom BU, Safran Morpho

Safran Morpho | morpho.com
Yves is Vice President & General Manager for the Telecom Business Unit at Morpho, a SAFRAN group company. He joined Morpho in March 2012, and is Board Director of the SIMalliance. He has significant experience in Marketing and Business Unit management within high tech markets.

Prior to this, Yves was heading R&D projects for fixed multimedia terminals, and investigated impacts of the internet on telecommunication terminals and services within Sagem.

   Empowering connectivity, security, and identity in the world of IoT
BUSINESS TRACK - 17h15/17h30
IoT is becoming a reality, thanks to watches, cars, alarms, smart grids… billions of connected objects will be deployed everywhere on the planet over the coming years!
This will create a real challenge for the OEMs to enable their services with the right connectivity at the right quality and cost, whatever the objects operational destination.
To enable this connected world, OEMs will need to use the next generation of SIM cards, securely enabling multiple Mobile Network Profiles and ubiquitous connectivity.
Yves will explore how this new Secure Element will equip the IoT object with security, used for the traditional communication interfaces and also the transactions it performs, in addition to new use cases which this new capability will create, and new infrastructure capabilities.
